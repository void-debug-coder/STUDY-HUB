📖 Module 1: Simple Stress & Strain - Key Notes

1. Introduction

The ability of element of the structure to resist its failure under external force is known as 'strength of material' and the ability to resist deformation is 'stiffness'.【915229974074058714†L22-L24】

2. Stress (σ)

The force of resistance per unit area, offered by a body against deformation. Stress = P/A where P = External force, A = Cross-sectional area.【915229974074058714†L178-L179】【915229974074058714†L183-L186】 Units: N/mm² or MPa. 1 N/mm² = 1 MN/m²【915229974074058714†L202-L203】

3. Types of Stress

Direct/Normal Stress: Due to normal force acting on bar. Tensile (+ve) or Compressive (-ve).【915229974074058714†L417-L421】

4. Strain (ε)

Measure of deformation produced in material due to applied load. Direct Strain = ΔL/L. Dimensionless.【915229974074058714†L442-L446】

5. Hooke's Law & Young's Modulus

Stress is proportional to strain up to elastic limit: σ ∝ ε → σ = Eε【915229974074058714†L340-L344】 E = Young's modulus or modulus of elasticity. Units: N/mm²【915229974074058714†L347-L348】

6. Stress-Strain Diagram - Salient Points

- Limit of Proportionality (A): Stress proportional to strain【915229974074058714†L322-L323】 - Elastic Limit: If unloaded, strain disappears completely【915229974074058714†L324-L326】 - Upper Yield Point (B): Load starts reducing, extension increases ~0.125% strain, ~250 N/mm²【915229974074058714†L327-L329】 - Ultimate Stress (D): Max stress material can resist ~370-400 N/mm²【915229974074058714†L332-L333】 - Breaking Point (E): Specimen fails. Strain 20-25%【915229974074058714†L336-L337】

7. Poisson's Ratio (μ)

Ratio of lateral strain to linear strain【915229974074058714†L228-L229】
