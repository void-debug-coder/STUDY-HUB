📖 Module 1: Stress & Strain - Key Notes

1. Introduction & Strength of Material

The ability of element of the structure to resist its failure under external force is known as 'strength of material' and the ability to resist deformation is 'stiffness'. Materials first deform, then failure takes place.

2. Stress (σ) = P/A

The force of resistance per unit area offered by a body against deformation. Units: N/mm² or MPa. Direct/Normal Stress: Tensile (+ve) or Compressive (-ve).

3. Strain (ε) = ΔL/L

Measure of deformation. Direct strain is dimensionless. Ratio of change in length to original length.

4. Hooke's Law: σ = Eε

Within elastic limit, stress ∝ strain. E = Young's modulus / modulus of elasticity. Units: N/mm² or GPa.

5. Stress-Strain Diagram - Key Points

- Limit of Proportionality: Stress ∝ strain - Elastic Limit: Strain disappears completely if unloaded - Upper Yield Point: ~250 N/mm² for mild steel, strain ~0.125% - Ultimate Stress: Max stress ~370-400 N/mm² - Breaking Point: Failure, strain 20-25%

6. Topic 2: Equilibrium of Forces

ΣF_x = 0, ΣF_y = 0, ΣM = 0. Free Body Diagrams isolate the body and show all external forces + reactions.

7. Topic 3: Support Reactions

Roller: 1 reaction ⊥ surface. Pin: 2 reactions. Fixed: 3 reactions. Solve using equilibrium equations.
