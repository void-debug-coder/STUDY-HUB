📖 Module 1: Simple Notes

1. What is Strength of Materials?

Simple: It studies how materials behave when you push, pull, or twist them.

Why? So buildings and bridges don't break.
  • Strength: How much force before it breaks
  • Stiffness: How much it bends/stretches

2. Stress = Force ÷ Area

Formula: σ = F / A
Units: N/mm² = MPa

Two Types:

  • Tensile Stress: Pulling apart. Like stretching a rubber band. +ve
  • Compressive Stress: Pushing together. Like squashing a can. -ve
  • Shear Stress: Sliding force. Like scissors cutting paper. τ = V / A
Example: 1000N force on 100mm² area → Stress = 1000/100 = 10 MPa

3. Strain = How Much It Stretches

Formula: ε = Change in Length / Original Length = ΔL / L
Units: No units. Just a number.
  • If a 1000mm bar stretches by 2mm → Strain = 2/1000 = 0.002
  • Tensile strain: Gets longer, +ve
  • Compressive strain: Gets shorter, -ve

4. Hooke's Law: Stress ∝ Strain

Formula: σ = E × ε
E = Young's Modulus - shows how stiff material is
  • Steel: E = 200,000 MPa - very stiff
  • Aluminum: E = 70,000 MPa - less stiff
  • Rubber: E = 10 MPa - very flexible
  • Bigger E = Harder to stretch

5. How Much Does It Stretch? δ = FL/AE

Formula: Deformation δ = Force × Length / Area × E

Meaning: More force = more stretch. Longer bar = more stretch.
Bigger area or stiffer material = less stretch.
Example: Steel bar, F=10,000N, L=2000mm, A=100mm², E=200,000MPa
δ = 10,000×2000 / 100×200,000 = 1mm stretch

6. Poisson's Ratio: Side Squeeze

Simple: When you pull a material, it gets thinner on the sides.
μ = Side strain / Pulling strain
  • Steel: μ = 0.3
  • Concrete: μ = 0.2
  • If μ = 0.3 and you stretch 0.001, sides shrink by 0.0003

7. Temperature Changes Cause Stress Too

If free to expand: δ = α × L × ΔT. No stress.
If blocked: Stress = E × α × ΔT
α: How much it expands per °C. Steel α = 12×10⁻⁶
Example: Steel blocked, heated 50°C → Stress = 200,000 × 12×10⁻⁶ × 50 = 120 MPa compression

8. Supports & Equilibrium

To stay still, forces must balance:

  • ΣF_x = 0: Left-right forces balance
  • ΣF_y = 0: Up-down forces balance
  • ΣM = 0: No spinning

Support Types:

  • Roller: 1 force, up/down only
  • Pin: 2 forces, up/down + left/right
  • Fixed: 3 reactions, up/down + left/right + moment

9. Key Numbers to Remember

Steel: E = 200 GPa, μ = 0.3, Yield = 250 MPa
Concrete: E = 25 GPa, μ = 0.2
Aluminum: E = 70 GPa, μ = 0.33
