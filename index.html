📐 DIRECT STRESS & STRAIN - FUNDAMENTALS

1. Direct Stress

Definition: Force per unit area acting perpendicular to the cross-section
Formula: σ = P/A
Units: N/mm², MPa, kN/m², Pa

Types:
1. Tensile Stress: Pulling force → Elongation → +σ
2. Compressive Stress: Pushing force → Shortening → -σ
Example 1: Simple Tensile Stress
A steel bar 50mm diameter carries an axial load of 100 kN. Find stress.

Solution:
A = π×50²/4 = 1963.5 mm²
σ = P/A = 100,000/1963.5 = 50.93 N/mm² (Tensile)

2. Direct Strain

Definition: Ratio of change in length to original length
Formula: ε = ΔL/L = δL/L
Units: Dimensionless, often expressed as % or με

Types:
1. Tensile Strain: +ε (increase in length)
2. Compressive Strain: -ε (decrease in length)
Example 2: Strain Calculation
A bar 2m long extends by 0.5mm under load. Find strain.

Solution:
ε = ΔL/L = 0.5/2000 = 0.00025 = 2.5×10⁻⁴
ε = 0.025% = 250 με

3. Hooke's Law & Young's Modulus

Hooke's Law: Within elastic limit, stress ∝ strain
σ ∝ ε → σ = E×ε

Young's Modulus E: Ratio of stress to strain
E = σ/ε = (P/A)/(ΔL/L) = P×L/(A×ΔL)
Units: N/mm², GPa, kN/mm²

Typical E values:
Steel: 200-210 GPa | Aluminum: 70 GPa | Brass: 100 GPa
Copper: 120 GPa | Concrete: 25-30 GPa
Example 3: Find E from Test Data
A bar 30mm dia, 200mm gauge length extends 0.12mm under 50kN load. Find E.

Solution:
A = π×30²/4 = 706.86 mm²
σ = 50,000/706.86 = 70.74 N/mm²
ε = 0.12/200 = 0.0006
E = σ/ε = 70.74/0.0006 = 117,900 N/mm² = 117.9 GPa

4. Factor of Safety

FOS: Ratio of ultimate/breaking stress to allowable working stress
FOS = σ_ultimate / σ_working = σ_yield / σ_allowable

Typical FOS: Buildings: 3-4, Bridges: 4-6, Machines: 6-12

5. Bars of Varying Cross-Section

Rule: Total elongation = Sum of elongation of each part
ΔL_total = Σ(P×L_i)/(A_i×E_i) = σ₁L₁/E₁ + σ₂L₂/E₂ + ...

If same P & E: ΔL = (P/E)(L₁/A₁ + L₂/A₂ + ...)
Example 4: Stepped Bar
A bar has section AB: L₁ = 500mm, A₁ = 1000mm² and BC: L₂ = 800mm, A₂ = 500mm².
Carries axial load P = 50 kN. E = 200 GPa. Find total elongation.

Solution:
σ₁ = P/A₁ = 50,000/1000 = 50 N/mm²
σ₂ = P/A₂ = 50,000/500 = 100 N/mm²

ΔL = σ₁L₁/E + σ₂L₂/E
ΔL = (50×500)/200,000 + (100×800)/200,000
ΔL = 0.125 + 0.4 = 0.525 mm
🔥 THERMAL STRESS & STRAIN

6. Thermal Stress - Basic Cases

Case 1: Free Expansion
ΔL_free = L×α×ΔT, ε = α×ΔT, σ = 0

Case 2: Fully Restrained
σ = E×α×ΔT, F = A×E×α×ΔT
Heating → Compression, Cooling → Tension

Case 3: Supports Yield by Δ
σ = E(α×ΔT - Δ/L)

7. Stepped Bars Fixed Ends

Rule: For stepped bar fixed at both ends, thermal force P is constant throughout.
Strain Compatibility: α×ΔT×ΣL = P/E × Σ(L_i/A_i)
Solve for P: P = E×α×ΔT×ΣL / Σ(L_i/A_i)
Then stresses: σ_i = P/A_i

8. Composite Bars - Different Materials

Conditions:
1. Strain Compatibility: α₁ΔT + σ₁/E₁ = α₂ΔT + σ₂/E₂
2. Force Equilibrium: A₁σ₁ + A₂σ₂ = 0 (no external load)

Solving: σ₁ = E₁E₂A₂(α₂-α₁)ΔT/(A₁E₂+A₂E₁)
Rule: Material with higher α goes into COMPRESSION

9. Rod & Tube Composite

Setup: Rod inside tube, screwed/nutted at ends. Heated ΔT.
Equations:
1. Force balance: A_rod×σ_rod + A_tube×σ_tube = 0
2. Strain: α_rod×ΔT + σ_rod/E_rod = α_tube×ΔT + σ_tube/E_tube

10. Thermal + External Load Combined

When composite carries load P + heated ΔT:
A₁σ₁ + A₂σ₂ = P
α₁ΔT + σ₁/E₁ = α₂ΔT + σ₂/E₂

Solution:
σ₁ = [P×E₁ + E₁E₂A₂(α₂-α₁)ΔT] / (A₁E₂+A₂E₁)
σ₂ = [P×E₂ + E₁E₂A₁(α₁-α₂)ΔT] / (A₁E₂+A₂E₁)

EXAM TIPS:
1. Always check units: E in N/mm² or GPa, α in /°C or /°F
2. Heating → Compression if restrained, Cooling → Tension
3. Higher α material → Compression in composites
4. For stepped bars, force P is constant, stress varies with 1/A
5. Always verify: Σ(A×σ) = External Load (or 0)
