📐 DIRECT STRESS & STRAIN - FUNDAMENTALS

1. Direct Stress

Definition: Force per unit area acting perpendicular to the cross-section
Formula: σ = P/A
Units: N/mm², MPa, kN/m², Pa

Types:
1. Tensile Stress: Pulling force → Elongation → +σ
2. Compressive Stress: Pushing force → Shortening → -σ
Example 1: Simple Tensile Stress
A steel bar 50mm diameter carries an axial load of 100 kN. Find stress.

Solution:
A = π×50²/4 = 1963.5 mm²
σ = P/A = 100,000/1963.5 = 50.93 N/mm² (Tensile)

2. Direct Strain

Definition: Ratio of change in length to original length
Formula: ε = ΔL/L = δL/L
Units: Dimensionless, often expressed as % or με

Types:
1. Tensile Strain: +ε (increase in length)
2. Compressive Strain: -ε (decrease in length)
Example 2: Strain Calculation
A bar 2m long extends by 0.5mm under load. Find strain.

Solution:
ε = ΔL/L = 0.5/2000 = 0.00025 = 2.5×10⁻⁴
ε = 0.025% = 250 με

3. Hooke's Law & Young's Modulus

Hooke's Law: Within elastic limit, stress ∝ strain
σ ∝ ε → σ = E×ε

Young's Modulus E: Ratio of stress to strain
E = σ/ε = (P/A)/(ΔL/L) = P×L/(A×ΔL)
Units: N/mm², GPa, kN/mm²

Typical E values:
Steel: 200-210 GPa | Aluminum: 70 GPa | Brass: 100 GPa
Copper: 120 GPa | Concrete: 25-30 GPa | Gun Metal: 100 GPa
Example 3: Find E from Test Data
A bar 30mm dia, 200mm gauge length extends 0.12mm under 50kN load. Find E.

Solution:
A = π×30²/4 = 706.86 mm²
σ = 50,000/706.86 = 70.74 N/mm²
ε = 0.12/200 = 0.0006
E = σ/ε = 70.74/0.0006 = 117,900 N/mm² = 117.9 GPa

4. Factor of Safety

FOS: Ratio of ultimate/breaking stress to allowable working stress
FOS = σ_ultimate / σ_working = σ_yield / σ_allowable

Typical FOS: Buildings: 3-4, Bridges: 4-6, Machines: 6-12

5. Bars of Varying Cross-Section

Rule: Total elongation = Sum of elongation of each part
ΔL_total = Σ(P×L_i)/(A_i×E_i) = σ₁L₁/E₁ + σ₂L₂/E₂ + ...

If same P & E: ΔL = (P/E)(L₁/A₁ + L₂/A₂ + ...)
Example 4: Stepped Bar
A bar has section AB: L₁ = 500mm, A₁ = 1000mm² and BC: L₂ = 800mm, A₂ = 500mm².
Carries axial load P = 50 kN. E = 200 GPa. Find total elongation.

Solution:
σ₁ = P/A₁ = 50,000/1000 = 50 N/mm²
σ₂ = P/A₂ = 50,000/500 = 100 N/mm²

ΔL = σ₁L₁/E + σ₂L₂/E
ΔL = (50×500)/200,000 + (100×800)/200,000
ΔL = 0.125 + 0.4 = 0.525 mm
🔥 THERMAL STRESS & STRAIN

6. Thermal Stress - Basic Cases

Case 1: Free Expansion
ΔL_free = L×α×ΔT, ε = α×ΔT, σ = 0

Case 2: Fully Restrained
σ = E×α×ΔT, F = A×E×α×ΔT
Heating → Compression, Cooling → Tension

Case 3: Supports Yield by Δ
σ = E(α×ΔT - Δ/L)

α Values: Steel: 12×10⁻⁶, Brass: 19×10⁻⁶, Al: 24×10⁻⁶, Gun Metal: 10×10⁻⁶, Concrete: 10×10⁻⁶ /°C or /°F
Example 5: Aluminum Bar Fixed Ends
An aluminum bar fixed at both ends is heated through 20K. Find stress developed.
Take E = 80 GPa = 80,000 N/mm², α = 24×10⁻⁶ /K

Solution:
Since fixed both ends, expansion prevented → Compressive stress
σ = E×α×ΔT
σ = 80,000 × 24×10⁻⁶ × 20
σ = 38.4 N/mm² (Compressive)
Example 6: Max Temperature Rise - Brass Rod
A brass rod 20m long is fixed at both ends. If thermal stress is not to exceed 76.5 N/mm², calculate the temperature through which the bar should be heated. Take α = 17×10⁻⁶ /°C, E = 90 kN/mm²

Given: L = 20m = 20,000mm, σ_max = 76.5 N/mm², α = 17×10⁻⁶ /°C, E = 90,000 N/mm²

Solution:
For fixed bar: σ = E×α×ΔT
76.5 = 90,000 × 17×10⁻⁶ × ΔT
76.5 = 1.53 × ΔT
ΔT = 50°C

Answer: Bar can be heated by 50°C maximum

7. Stepped Bars Fixed Ends - FROM YOUR NOTES

Rule: For stepped bar fixed at both ends, thermal force P is constant throughout.

Strain Compatibility: Total free expansion = Total contraction by force P
α×ΔT×(L₁+L₂+...) = P/E × (L₁/A₁ + L₂/A₂ + ...)

Solve for P: P = E×α×ΔT×(L₁+L₂) / (L₁/A₁ + L₂/A₂ + ...)

Then stresses: σ₁ = P/A₁, σ₂ = P/A₂, etc.
Key: Smaller area → Higher thermal stress
Example 7: Stepped Steel Rod - FROM YOUR NOTES
Steel rod AB firmly held between rigid supports. A₁=400mm², L₁=500mm, A₂=600mm², L₂=200mm. Heated 15K. Find stress. α=12×10⁻⁶/°C, E=200 GPa

Solution:
Step 1: Free expansion
ΔL_free = α×ΔT×(L₁+L₂) = 12×10⁻⁶×15×700 = 0.126mm

Step 2: Force P to prevent expansion
0.126 = P/200,000 × (500/400 + 200/600)
0.126 = P/200,000 × 1.5833
P = 15,916 N

Step 3: Stresses
σ₁ = 15,916/400 = 39.8 N/mm² (Compressive)
σ₂ = 15,916/600 = 26.5 N/mm² (Compressive)

That formula only works for uniform cross-section. For stepped, you MUST use compatibility: P = E×α×ΔT×ΣL / Σ(L/A)

8. Thermal Stresses in Composite Bars - YOUR HANDWRITTEN NOTES

FROM YOUR NOTES: Whenever there is some increase or decrease in temp of a bar consisting of two or more different materials it cause the bar to contract or expand.

On account of different coefficients of linear expansion, the two materials do not expand or contract by the same amount but by different amounts.

Case 1: If both materials expand freely, no stress produced.
Case 2: If materials are rigidly fixed, composite expands as a whole by same amount.

Result: Material with higher α gets COMPRESSED. Material with lower α gets TENSION.

Example 8: Steel Core in Brass Tube - FROM NOTES

11. Thermal + External Load Combined - MISSING CONCEPT

When composite carries load P + heated ΔT:
A₁σ₁ + A₂σ₂ = P (not 0!)
α₁ΔT + σ₁/E₁ = α₂ΔT + σ₂/E₂

Solution:
σ₁ = [P×E₁ + E₁E₂A₂(α₂-α₁)ΔT] / (A₁E₂+A₂E₁)
σ₂ = [P×E₂ + E₁E₂A₁(α₁-α₂)ΔT] / (A₁E₂+A₂E₁)

Superposition: Total stress = Load stress + Thermal stress

Case Formula Notes
1. Direct Stress σ = P/A Basic tension/compression
2. Direct Strain ε = ΔL/L = σ/E Hooke's Law
3. Young's Modulus E = σ/ε = P×L/(A×ΔL) Stiffness measure
4. Varying Section ΔL = Σ(P×L_i)/(A_i×E_i) Sum of parts
5. Free Expansion ΔL = L×α×ΔT, σ = 0 No stress
6. Fully Restrained σ = E×α×ΔT Max thermal stress
7. Supports Yield Δ σ = E(α×ΔT - Δ/L) Reduced stress
8. Stepped Fixed P = E×α×ΔT×ΣL / Σ(L/A) Force constant, σ = P/A
9. Composite 2-Mat σ₁ = E₁E₂A₂(α₂-α₁)ΔT/(A₁E₂+A₂E₁) Higher α → compression
10. Rod + Tube Same as composite A_tube = π/4(OD²-ID²)
11. Thermal + Load P σ₁ = [P×E₁ + E₁E₂A₂(α₂-α₁)ΔT]/(A₁E₂+A₂E₁) Superposition
12. Force Balance A₁σ₁ + A₂σ₂ = P (or = 0) Always check
